Forward

This project originated from my research — ‘Pain-diagram Assisted Survey Platform for Pain Management’. Doctors usually use Margolis Pain-diagram (Margolis, 1986) as a pain-recording tool to collect patients’ pain information. The Margolis Pain-diagram is a 2D human-body diagram which is divided into 43 body blocks, so patients can choose which part of their body feels pain and mark on the diagram. Then, doctors will analyse the cause of the pain and give patients treatment on time. My research discusses how to move this diagram on an electronic device, as a user-friendly interactive application, to provide better help for the medical treatment process.

Methods used in the research:
Questionnaire user research
Face to face interview
Persona
User journey map
Wireframing
User flow
SUS analysis

Sift appropriate participants & research potential user requirements

In user research phase, the first step is to find appropriate participants from schoolmates, teachers, community friends and people in social media groups. Sifting out participants who have experience of chronic disease and letting them to join in the research, because patients who have or had chronic disease are user groups who have the strongest motivation, and they will be more positive in the research.


There are 31 questionnaires received in the end, and 5 participants had chronic disease in the past. I made appointments with them individually for a face to face interview. Before interviews, the web app was wire-framed on several papers, in this way, these wireframes can imitate the real application which can be tested by participants during their interviews.

There were conclusions drawn after interviews:

  • Participants think they can understand the purpose of this application design, but their chronic pain is always diagnosed by doctors, so they think it’s unnecessary to be collected by individuals.
  • Some participants think the function of this application is too simple to help them understand causes of their pain.
  • All 5 participants think the way of choosing pain location on the pain-diagram is rough. While the detailed body structures are too professional for them to distinguish, if they are not clear about the body structure, they will tend to choose a wrong location, which may lead to an inaccurate pain record.


Reflect on the first design

The first version of my design was a web app. After entering the application, users can add a pain record and mark their pain regions on the pain-diagram. Then, choosing a type of the pain and other attributes in the form followed. In the end, submitting the record to save it in the backend. At the meantime, the data visualisation charts like bar charts can provide reference of pain severity through this period of the symptom. However, the result of the System Usability Scale analysis did not show obviously that the application was usable, and testers also agreed with that this application was more suitable for back-end pain management rather than used by end users. Therefore, the first design was seen as failed.


Rethinking how pain areas should be marked

In 2D body diagram, simple marks cannot locate the pain areas accurately. There was an idea which was adding grid on the pain-diagram, but the 2D diagram has no body details for reference, so it did not let participants draw their pain more accurately. In some example, participants were more confused conversely.


In the second design process, it was considered that the entire-block-marking method led to a drawback of rough recording, while patients were very clear about their symptoms and the type of pain, so they should have been given more chance to provide these information.

For how to display these marks, usually, different types of brush are used to draw various touches in computer painting. Inspired by this, I designed several labels according to different symptoms as marking tools, which was more objective than the original way.


The diversity of different user groups

Participants mostly consist of local British and oversea students. It has been found during the interview that, under the current British medial system, different people have different ways of dealing with physical pain.
(The UK healthcare policy is free. Patients need to make an appointment with GP, which is a community doctor. Then, they can go to a hospital to receive further treatment if they get the recommendation of their GP. This process typically lasts a long period of time.)

  1. Some locals choose to take the advantage of free medical care, which is booking a GP examination. Then, they wait for a long time to go to a public hospital.
  2. Some locals with strong financial strength tend to choose efficient and expansive private hospital.
  3. Oversea student generally do not like to go to hospital or GP, sometimes they don’t even pay enough attention to their pain.


Analyse pain points of different user groups

According to personas and results of interview, analysing possible problems and solutions that the diversity of user groups points.
Users, like Dan and Lucy, have two different feedback when they feel pain on their body:

  1. Lucy is an oversea student. She is not familiar with local environment, which may cause some complicated and overloaded issue, when she seeks medical help. It means she tends to bear the pain and do self observation. Therefore, she needs more information and reference in this stage.
  2. Dan is older, and he get used to contact private doctors. Because of the chronic pain all year around, he would like to seek medical help immediately.

This represents the different pain points of the two types of users: One needs efficient and reliable private medical care; the other requires handy tips, analysis and health guidelines of their disease.


The process of recording

Understanding users are not clear about physical location of their pain actually. Because the data patients provided are limited, which is limited in area, type, severity and visible or not, so the process of submitting the form should be as short as possible. In this way, confusion can be reduced and the mistake in the first design can be avoided.

Analyze potential disease, recommend medications and locate nearby pharmacies

For users like Lucy, after pain data are submitted, a report will be sent back and it will help themselves to analyse pain and their symptoms. In addition, over-the-counter drugs which can help alleviate the pain are recommended and the pharmacies nearby where the drug can be purchased are located.

Providing clinic options list & medical appointments

For users like Dan, because they are willing to contact a doctor directly, the method to meet their requirements is to locate nearby quality clinics and make online appointment with doctors, which can help them see a doctor rapidly.

Allow doctors and users communicate and assist

There was a problem that messages were hidden in the communication interface. If a user want to look through the conversation with a doctor, he has to click the button in the appointment interface. However, messages from a doctor can be very important, and the application should allow users to see it directly, so my design below helps to achieve this goal.

Review

  • More tests are required

    In the usability test for the first design, participants confused by functions of the application, so they felt inaccurate to handle some questions in the SUS form. Since the System Usability Scale is a test of overall rough usability, it is better to combine with the observation method and the A/B test method.

  • Detailed interaction design & information structure are needed

    The second design solved main user cases. However, there is a lack of detailed interaction design to inform all interactions between every operation. In addition, because the information structure was not pre-designed in this case, some of the information that emerges in the application may not be the best solution in the criteria of information architecture.

  • The graphic design can be improved further

    The graphic design is relatively simple, which can be optimised in the future according to the preferences and feedback of users. In addition, icons, text, button size and typography can also be more detailed and standardised.
